Reflective Journal | PPW Module

A reflection on the core concepts learned during the Preparing for Professional World (PPW) lectures.

1. Professional Presentation Skills

Reflections on Effective Presentations: Designing impactful slides requires visual balance. A common trap is text stuffing; instead, one must use interesting visual aids that don't distract. The presenter remains the critical element—there must be a seamless match between the slide content and the spoken words.

  • Concluding: Always provide a strong closing message rather than an abrupt end.
  • Handling Q&A: Utilize a dedicated slide to signal transitions, maintaining visual aids to support answers.
  • References: Consistently include references for any research presented.

2. Professional Interview Etiquette

Bridging the Gap in Remote Interviews: Remote interviews demand the same level of preparation as face-to-face ones. Professional attire is mandatory. Punctuality is achieved by settling in 10 minutes early to ensure the technical setup is flawless.

  • Communication: Apply the "Small Gap" rule—pause briefly after questions to prevent internet-lag interruptions.
  • Transparency: Be technically transparent; inform interviewers immediately of audio issues.
  • Closing: Always express gratitude and follow up on the next steps.

3. Skills Development & Communication

Practice and Etiquette: Confidence stems from practice. Role-playing with peers is a proven method to simulate professional call pressure. Communication etiquette, especially telephone skills, emphasizes professional introductions and the courtesy of updating hosts regarding delays.

4. Professional Report Writing

Focus and Objectivity: Workplace reports must maintain a strictly objective tone. Personal conflicts must be excluded or handled with professional distance. The primary focus is providing the audience with targeted, relevant information.

  • Structure: Complex data, graphs, and large tables belong in Appendices to maximize the main document's fluidity.

5. Formal Email & Memo Writing

Modern Correspondence: Emails are efficient, cost-effective, and historically crucial (as seen during the pandemic). Memos remain vital for reliable internal communications and organizational documentation.
